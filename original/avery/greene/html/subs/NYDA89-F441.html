Robert R. Blacker House (Pasadena, Calif.). Billiard Room
Details of billiard room, residence for R. R. Blacker, Esq. at Oak Knoll, Cal. / Greene & Greene, Architects. 1909 12 sheets various media 59.6 x 101.4 cm. (23 1/2 x 40 in.) or smaller

Many of the drawings are severely damaged.
Job no. 250.
"Because of the lengthy period of careful maintenance and restoration by the present owners, Mr. and Mrs. Max Hill, the structure has, in recent years, been frequently identified as the Blacker-Hill house" -- Greene & Greene, architecture as a fine, by Randell L. Makinson, Salt Lake City, Peregrine Smith, 1977, p. 150.
Date from inventory.
The set consists at 3 blueprints with crayon markings on paper, 3 pencil drawings with crayon on tracing paper, 2 blueprints on paper, 2 pencil drawings on tracing paper, 1 ink drawing with ink wash on cloth, and 1 ink drawing on cloth.
Scale 1/4" = 1' or 1 1/2" = 1' or full size.
