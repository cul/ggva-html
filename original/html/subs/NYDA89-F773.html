Norman Kerry House (Beverly Hills, Calif.). Garden
Design for a garden for Mr. Norman Kerry at Beverly Hills, [Calif.] / Henry M. Greene, Architect. 1922 24 sheets various media 54 x 89 cm. (21 3/8 x 35 in.) or smaller

Scale varies.
Job no. 443.
"At this time the original Earle C. Anthony house (1909) was set for demolition to allow for the construction of a multi-story apartment building when it was rescued by the distinguished actor of the silent screen, Norman Kerry. He and his wife had the house moved in three sections to Beverly Hills and engaged Henry Greene to handle the resiting and rejoining of the structure and to design the grounds and gardens." -- from Greene & Greene, architecture as a fine art, by Randell L. Makinson, Salt Lake City, 1977, p. 242.
This set consists of 15 pencil drawings on tracing paper, 3 pencil and ink drawings on tracing paper, 3 ink drawings with pencil markings on cloth, 1 sepia process print on paper, 1 blue line print with pencil markings on paper, 1 ink drawing with pencil markings on tracing paper, and 1 blueprint on paper.
