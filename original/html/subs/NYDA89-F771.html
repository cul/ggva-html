Cordelia A. Culbertson House (Pasadena, Calif.). Grounds and Outbuildings
Greene, Henry Mather, 1870-1954.
Potting house, retaining wall, [etc.], for Mr. Francis F. Prentiss, 1188 Hillcrest Ave., Pasadena, [Calif.] / Henry M. Greene, Architect. 1931 18 sheets various media 50.8 x 77.1 cm. (20 x 30 3/8 in.) or smaller

Most of the drawings are encapsulated in mylar. Many are severely damaged.
Job no. 514.
Set includes two booklets, typewritten original and carbon copy, entitled "Specifications for a one story frame and masonry potting house for Mr. Francis F. Prentiss, Pasadena, California, June 1931, Henry M. Greene, Architect, job no. 514". Original booklet is stamped "Set no. 1, office copy", copy stamped "Set no. 3". Both have 4 pages and neither was accessioned or photographed.
This set consists of 13 pencil drawings on tracing paper, 2 pencil drawings on paper, 1 pencil and crayon drawing on tracing paper, 1 blueprint on paper, and 1 blueprint with crayon markings on paper.
"Charles and Henry were each involved in various additional designs for Mrs. Francis F. Prentiss who had now become as much identified with the Cordelia A. Culbertson house as the Culbertsons themselves" -- Greene & Greene, architecture as a fine art, by Randell L. Makinson, Salt Lake City, Peregrine Smith, 1977, p. 261.
Scale varies.
Set also includes a sheet of stationery from the "Williams Radiator Co." with calculations on it and a sheet of stationery from "Edwars H. Rust, Nurseryman" with notes about plants on the verso. Neither of these was accessioned nor photographed.
